Abstract Contemporary counterfeiting techniques have evolved beyond simple visual imitation, requiring advanced forensic methods for detection. This research presents the "AI Currency Guardian," a high-precision analysis engine that moves beyond basic structural checks. By integrating texture density analysis for watermarks, Hough-line continuity for security threads, Laplacian variance for intaglio printing, OCR for microprint, and HSV histogram analysis for Optically Variable Ink (OVI), we establish a comprehensive 5-tier verification protocol. This multi-stream forensic approach ensures that even high-quality counterfeits are identified through subtle physical and digital discrepancies.

Keywords: Forensic Vision, Texture Analysis, Intaglio Detection, OVI Analysis, Counterfeit Intelligence.

1. Introduction

Modern banknotes incorporate a variety of security features designed to be easily verified by humans but difficult to replicate by machines. Our "AI Currency Guardian" reverses this paradigm by using computer vision to outperform manual inspection. The system targets five specific physical properties: the transparency of watermarks, the continuity of metallic threads, the tactile depth of intaglio ink, the legibility of micro-text, and the spectral shift of OVI.

2. Forensic Methodology

The system employs five parallel processing streams, each dedicated to a specific security feature. This modularity allows for granular strictness adjustment and detailed error reporting.

2.1 Watermark (Texture Density)

Authentic watermarks are embedded within the paper pulp, creating varying pixel densities under backlighting. We use Gaussian blurring followed by binary thresholding to measure "Density Fill." A genuine note exhibits a specific range of pixel transparency that surface-level printing cannot replicate.

2.2 Security Thread (Line Continuity)

The metallic thread is a continuous structural element. Using Canny edges and Probabilistic Hough Line Transform, the model isolates vertical structures. A "Broken" or scattered line detection indicates a surface-printed imitation of the thread.

2.3 Intaglio (Edge Sharpness & Variance)

Intaglio printing involves high-pressure ink application, creating a unique texture. We apply a Laplacian operator to compute the second-order derivative of gray levels. High variance in this stream confirms the presence of "Raised Ink" textures found on genuine currency.

2.4 Microprinting (OCR Verification)

Micro-text like "BANGLADESH BANK" is often too small for common printers to render clearly. Using PyTesseract with customized configurations, the system attempts to match specific keywords. If the text is unrecognizable or "Blurred," the feature fails the authenticity check.

2.5 OVI (Color Histogram Mapping)

Optically Variable Ink changes color based on the viewing angle. By mapping the banknote to the HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) color space and analyzing the variance in the color histogram, we can detect the "Color Shift" property that standard CMYK printers lack.

3. Scoring & Verdict Logic
